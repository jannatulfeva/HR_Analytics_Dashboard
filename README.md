# HR_Analytics_Dashboard

An interactive Power BI project designed to analyze employee demographics and turnover trends to identify the primary drivers of attrition.

# Overview
This project transforms raw human resources data into a diagnostic tool that tracks workforce stability. The goal is to identify why employees leave the organization and which departments are at the highest risk. Using Power BI, the analysis provides a granular view of turnover across various segments like age, salary, and job role to support data-driven retention strategies.

# Business Objective
The main objectives of this project are:

- Track key workforce KPIs (Total Employees, Attrition Rate, Average Age).

- Identify high-risk attrition clusters by age group and salary bracket.

- Analyze turnover patterns across different job roles and education fields.

- Compare attrition rates between genders and experience levels.

- Provide actionable insights to help HR management reduce employee turnover.

# Dataset
The project uses a comprehensive HR dataset (processed via Excel) containing employee profiles and employment history.
Important columns include:

- Attrition: (Yes/No status)

- Age & Gender: Demographic identifiers.

- Job Role: Specific position within the company.

- Monthly Income: Salary data for compensation analysis.

- Total Working Years: Experience level.

- Education Field: Academic background of the workforce.

# Tools & Technologies
The analysis and visualization were performed using:

- Power BI: For data modeling, DAX calculations, and interactive dashboarding.

- Excel: For initial data storage and structured formatting.

- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): To create custom measures for attrition rates and averages.

# Data Cleaning & Preparation
Before building the dashboard, the following steps were completed:

- Standardized salary and age data into "bins" (e.g., Age Groups, Salary Ranges) for better categorization.

- Handled missing values and verified data consistency across records.

- Created calculated measures for Attrition Count and Attrition Rate.

- Optimized the data model to ensure fast, interactive filtering across all visuals.

# Key Findings
The analysis revealed several critical drivers of employee turnover:

- High-Risk Age Group: Employees aged 26–35 show the highest frequency of attrition.

- Compensation Impact: Turnover is significantly higher in the lower salary groups, suggesting a link between pay and retention.

- Departmental Hotspots: The Sales Executive and Laboratory Technician roles experience disproportionately high attrition compared to other roles.

- Experience Trends: Attrition tends to peak among employees with fewer years at the company, highlighting the need for better onboarding.


# Final Recommendations
Based on the dashboard insights, the following actions are recommended:

- Targeted Retention: Focus retention efforts on the 26–35 age demographic through career development programs.

- Salary Benchmarking: Review compensation for Sales and Lab roles to ensure market competitiveness.

- Engagement Surveys: Conduct stay-interviews for employees in the "low salary" and "early tenure" brackets.

- Work-Life Initiatives: Investigate the specific stressors in high-attrition roles to improve the work environment.
